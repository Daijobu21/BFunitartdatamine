Episode 4
—The Fall of Zeruiah—


Since the gift of Fire was introduced to the humans, their weaponry had improved by leaps and bounds.

To defend against any future incursions from otherworldly entities, they rallied their soldiers with hearts of iron and fists of steel.

The courts have long ceded their authority to their militaristic arms;

for many nobles, they sought only protection from threats both inside and out, and little else.

For others, the immense sway one man holds over the kingdoms is cause for concern...

The blacksteeled facade of the Empire's citadel rises up before Baelfyr—a silent, ironclad sentinel.

Standing before this imposing structure, he retrieves a small object from his pocket.

The bit of antler he had broken off Zeruiah glimmers softly, as if it too pulses with a warmth and life of its own.

Baelfyr
This is the beginning of the end for all of us.

Baelfyr
No one can stop me now.

He plunges the sharp end into his chest, biting his lip to stifle the hiss of pain welling in his throat.

As the wound begins to drip, he peels back his shirt to reveal the darkened, almost burned, flesh near his heart.

Baelfyr
(This will probably never heal.)

Baelfyr
(...Such is human sin; it pervades us all, and my soul the blackest among them.)

Baelfyr
(But it doesn't matter. I will take them down myself.)

He glares at the towering structure before placing his hand over the wound and slipping into its iron gates.

Empire Soldier
Sir...Sir Baelfyr!

Empire Soldier
He's injured! Someone, get a medic!

Empire General
What happened? This grievous wound...

Baelfyr
The Fae Sage...has turned.

Baelfyr
The corruption has never truly left the Vale. It has been left to fester.

Baelfyr
I shudder to think if the sage herself were to succumb to this madness.

Baelfyr
We must do something about this.

Empire General
True. We cannot allow our people to be threatened...

Baelfyr
The divine flame will light our path.

Baelfyr
It must be done...if we want to survive.

Court Advisor
This is madness!

Court Advisor
We cannot simply wage war on the words of one man!

Court Advisor
We should investigate the cause of the Sage's folly instead of attacking the weakened Fae!

Magistrate
Our cities are safe thanks to the swift actions of Sir Baelfyr.

Magistrate
What have YOU done, hmm? Will we sit here and wait for them to swallow our lands, then?

Empire Soldier
You and your nobles sit up in your ivory towers while the rest of us suffer!

Empire Footman
Who are the ones ankle-deep in blood, stepping over the bodies of the fallen, huh?!

Empire General
Enough!

Empire General
... ...

Empire General
I trust you to do what is right for our people.

Baelfyr
Of course.

Baelfyr
It is the only purpose of my life...

Treading vaguely remembered paths into the Vale brings little comfort to those who fought in the Voidborn invasion.

The dark, foreboding forests still echo with the screeching of their otherworldly enemies.

Despite the naturally accelerated healing of plant life, the ambience lends only an air of tension to the area.

Baelfyr
There are no screeching fiends to be found here, but their evil permeates the very earth.

Baelfyr
Will we allow this wound to fester and seep into our kingdoms?

Empire Soldiers
NEVER!

Baelfyr
Then in the name of the Holy Flame, purge this land!

Baelfyr
The darkness shall never hold sway over us.

With a rousing roar, the Empire's soldiers charge forth into the forest.

Baelfyr
Let them burn.

Baelfyr
Let them all burn...
