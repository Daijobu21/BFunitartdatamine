Final Episode
—Kismet—


An impenetrable silence hangs over the persistent gloom of the drowning plateaus.

Even the waves ceased their echoing roars, as if the Endless Abyss reflected the grief of their goddess.

Grief that spiralled rapidly into anguish, as she wrenched up the limp body of her High Priest.

Tsovinar
Sae...ren...this...

You were insolent, I believed. Worse than the cowering land-walkers above.

How quickly they fell over themselves to gain my favor...

Content to lie in my darkness, that was all I granted.

Here and there I heard something. Some attempt to communicate...some ebb and flow of energy.

But as long as they made their offerings, their affairs were below my concern.

...Then, you had the gall to wade into my domain.

The stagnant sea and my own restlessness gnawed at my patience, and so did you.

...but you never stopped coming.

Tsovinar
This talk of priests and their treacherous ways...Impossible!

Tsovinar
I am a *goddess*! How dare they keep me chained with false pretenses of worship!

Tsovinar
I will have them PUNISHED.

I spared you for your intervention. But only just.

Little did I know I had already understood...

...how important you had become.

For the first time since she had come into being, Tsovinar felt...pain.

Physical pain was of no concern to the deep goddess, but mental—indeed emotional—pain was foreign to her alien mind.

As Tsovinar's pallid face contorts in confusion and anger, she snaps up a hand toward that insolent dragon spirit.

The grievous wound left by her pelagic lance began to glow and fill his blood with a sickly green light.

The deep goddess looms over him and growls, eldritch accent filled with malice.

Tsovinar
You have deprived his congregation of their priest...and my swarm of his service!

Tsovinar
I will utterly *crush* you.

Tsovinar
Beg. Scream. There will be no mercy.

Tsovinar
Mnahn' hlirgh...Ah'mglw'nafh!

The cry that tore itself from the dragon spirit's throat was one of pure, choking agony.

The mark of the Abyss begins to burn his life force away as the tides surge with renewed force.

The Naga, witnessing their omnipotent goddess in the flesh, descend into a religious frenzy like no other.

The overrun beach, no longer held back by the warrior monk who single-handedly rebelled against the black tides,

mirrors the dark sky pierced by streaks of lightning and the rumble of thunder.

A hint of jade-green flashes amid the chaos, perhaps only a trick of the light.

The Queen Mother was nowhere to be seen, such was the force of the deep goddess's ire.

Reeling from the sudden cry, Sero-Anya whips around and watches in full horror the slow torture of her adoptive guardian.

Hopeless in the struggles against even her own selves, Sero-Anya nevertheless dives into the fray,

twin desires of destruction and salvation clinging to her tenuous grasp on identity.

The maelstrom rises, and blackest destiny begins to descend upon Aimyhr...

...once again victim of a goddess's unbridled fury.

The Endless Abyss hungers and all will drown.
